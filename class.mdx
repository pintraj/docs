# Class Management

Class management is a core feature of School CRM that allows you to create and organize classes based on different mediums, streams, shifts, and sections. This system helps you structure your academic programs effectively and manage student groups efficiently.

## What are Classes?

Classes in School CRM represent organized groups of students studying together under specific academic parameters. Each class is defined by its medium of instruction, stream, shift, and section, creating a structured learning environment.

### Class Components

- **Medium**: Language of instruction (English, Hindi, etc.)
- **Stream**: Academic specialization (Science, Commerce, Arts)
- **Shift**: Timing schedule (Morning, Afternoon, etc.)
- **Section**: Subdivision within a class (A, B, C, etc.)

## Class Configuration Options

### Medium Selection

Choose the appropriate medium of instruction:

#### English Medium
- **Primary Language**: English
- **Subjects**: All subjects taught in English
- **Additional Languages**: Hindi/Foreign languages as additional subjects

#### Hindi Medium
- **Primary Language**: Hindi
- **Subjects**: All subjects taught in Hindi
- **Additional Languages**: English as a compulsory subject

#### Bilingual Medium
- **Primary Languages**: Both English and Hindi
- **Subject Distribution**: Mix of languages based on subject
- **Language Policy**: Clear guidelines for language usage

### Stream Assignment (Optional)

If your school uses academic streams:

- **Science Stream**: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology
- **Commerce Stream**: Business Studies, Accountancy, Economics
- **Arts Stream**: Literature, History, Geography, Political Science
- **No Stream**: General education without specialization

### Shift Assignment (Optional)

Assign timing schedules:

- **Morning Shift**: Early classes (6:00 AM - 2:00 PM)
- **Regular Shift**: Standard hours (8:00 AM - 4:00 PM)
- **Afternoon Shift**: Late classes (12:00 PM - 8:00 PM)
- **No Specific Shift**: Flexible timing

### Section Management

Create and manage sections within classes:

#### Section Naming
- **Alphabetic**: A, B, C, D, etc.
- **Numerical**: 1, 2, 3, 4, etc.
- **Descriptive**: Alpha, Beta, Gamma, etc.

#### Section Configuration
- **Capacity**: Maximum students per section
- **Teachers**: Assign class teachers to sections
- **Rooms**: Assign specific classrooms to sections

## Class Types and Examples

### Primary Classes (Grades 1-5)
**Medium**: Usually English or Hindi
**Stream**: No streams at this level
**Sections**: A, B, C (typically 3-4 sections)
**Focus**: Basic education and skill development

### Middle Classes (Grades 6-8)
**Medium**: English or Hindi
**Stream**: Introduction to basic streams
**Sections**: A, B, C, D (4-5 sections)
**Focus**: Subject specialization introduction

### Secondary Classes (Grades 9-10)
**Medium**: English or Hindi
**Stream**: Science, Commerce, Arts
**Sections**: A, B, C (3-4 sections per stream)
**Focus**: Stream-based specialization

### Senior Secondary (Grades 11-12)
**Medium**: English or Hindi
**Stream**: Advanced Science, Commerce, Arts
**Sections**: A, B (2-3 sections per stream)
**Focus**: Career-oriented education

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue**: Cannot create class with selected parameters
**Solution**: Check that all required fields are filled and parameters are valid

**Issue**: Students cannot be assigned to class
**Solution**: Verify class capacity and enrollment status

**Issue**: Class schedule conflicts
**Solution**: Review teacher availability and room assignments

**Issue**: Section capacity exceeded
**Solution**: Either increase section capacity or create additional sections
